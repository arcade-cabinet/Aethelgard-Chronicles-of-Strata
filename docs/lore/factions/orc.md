# The Ember Highlanders

## Origin

Long after the charter, a delegation of strata-miners drove their
shafts through the **Verdant Stratum** and into the **Ember Stratum**
below — the first time anyone had touched fire-stone directly. The
result was not catastrophe but **binding**: the heat of the Ember
layer fused to their tools, then to their forges, then to their
blood. Their grandchildren were broader, redder, and hot to the
touch. They built their crimson-banner clans on the highlands above
the breach, where the strata vents.

The Ember Highlanders never signed the charter. They do not believe
in borders. They believe in *pressure* — that a realm's worth is
proved by what it can extract from its neighbours under threat.

## Disposition

**Pressure-applying.** An orc-archetype faction prefers **raid +
tribute** over occupation. A raid that returns with loot is a win;
a raid that takes the keep is an *embarrassment* (now they have to
administer the place).

An orc AI tends to:

- Skip the survey phase. Move toward the closest enemy frontier
  fast.
- Demand tribute early via the diplomacy system; the demand IS the
  move, not the war.
- Build raid-capable units (the existing v0.5 "raid" military
  archetype) over garrison units.
- Favour `frontier-raid` mode in matchmaking — it's where they're
  most comfortable.

## Signature units + buildings

| Unit / Building | Lore role |
|---|---|
| **Palace** | The forge-hold. Built around an Ember-vent. The brazier in the medieval keep is a polite fire; the orc hold's brazier is a *vent*. |
| **Footman (raider variant)** | Lightly armoured, fast; carries a hatchet and a torch. The torch is for setting the enemy's wood-store on fire on the way out. |
| **Watchtower** | Less common — the highlanders don't watch, they listen for hooves. |
| **Wall** | Built rarely; when they build a wall it's around the forge, not the perimeter. |
| **Wonder** | Forge-wonder. A vent so large it pulls heat from three strata at once. |

## Banner

Crimson over black, the field charged with a triangular crossed-axe
sigil (two raider hatchets at 60°).

## In-game flavour hooks

- **Procedural nickname**: preferred motifs are *Forge*, *Vent*,
  *Raid*, *Spark*, *Crucible*. Example: "THE LAUGHING CRUCIBLE" for
  an orc win via repeated tribute extraction.
- **MYTH event reactions**: an orc realm interprets wildfires as
  *the Ember Stratum approving*; quakes as *the Glacial Stratum
  complaining about the noise*.
- **Diplomacy**: opens with a demand. Treaties are kept only as
  long as they're profitable.
