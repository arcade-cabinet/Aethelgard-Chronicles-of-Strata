# The Charter Realms

## Origin

When the first Chronicler-Kings drew the hex-grid across Aethelgard,
they signed it in gold leaf — the **Aethelgard Charter**. Every
medieval-archetype faction is descended from one of the charter
signatories, and they have never let the chronicle forget it. The
charter is why they call themselves *kingdoms* and why every keep is
expected to keep records.

The medieval realms built the first **Keeps** (the player Palace
becomes one in fiction), the first **standing armies** (Footmen are
their household oath-bound), and the first **walls** (every wall is
mortared with strata-dust because the charter says walls must root
to the layer beneath them).

## Disposition

**Lawful expansion.** A medieval realm wants firm, surveyed borders;
formal pacts with named clauses; named wars with declared casus belli.
A medieval AI tends to:

- Open a match by surveying — exploiting nearby tiles, building
  watchtowers along the cartographic frontier.
- Propose pacts before raids; the pact is the move, not the war.
- Build wonders — wonders are the chronicle made stone.
- Be poor at sudden tactical shifts. They march in formation.

## Signature units + buildings

| Unit / Building | Lore role |
|---|---|
| **Palace** | The Keep of a Chronicler-King. Houses the brazier whose oath the Footmen swore. |
| **Footman** | Oath-bound household soldier. Gold pauldron is the oath-mark. |
| **Watchtower** | Surveying post — sees by strata-resonance. Charter requirement: every claimed tile must be visible from a tower. |
| **Wall** | Strata-dust mortared masonry. Roots to the layer below. |
| **Wonder** | Renaissance-tier covenant. Earns the realm a permanent line in the chronicle. |

## Banner

Gold leaf on obsidian, the field charged with the realm's hex-sigil
(a stylised render of the realm's home tile silhouette).

## In-game flavour hooks

- **Procedural nickname** (in GameOverModal `matchNickname`): preferred
  motifs are *Hearth*, *Keep*, *Charter*, *Brazier*, *Seal*. Example:
  "THE PATIENT HEARTH" for a long-defensive medieval win.
- **MYTH event reactions**: a medieval realm interprets quakes as
  *the strata reaffirming the charter*; wildfires as *the Ember
  Stratum disputing a claim*.
- **Diplomacy**: opens with a formal greeting; closes treaties via
  named ceremonies in chat lines.
